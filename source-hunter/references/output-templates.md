# Output Templates

## Quick / General

Use for default requests and "通用精选".

1. `TL;DR source map`: 3-5 sentences.
2. `Layered source list`: 8-12 sources total, grouped as L1-L4.
3. Each source: title; author/institution + year + freshness; identifier or official URL; one-line relevance.
4. `Search suggestions`: 2-3 exact queries and platforms.
5. `Next use`: one sentence.

## Citation Repair

Use for "补引用", "加引用", "citation needed", or source repair.

Prioritize exact identifiers and quote-safe bibliographic data. Keep commentary short.

Per source:

- Claim it supports.
- Full source title.
- Author/institution, year.
- DOI/arXiv/PubMed/official URL.
- Confidence and any caveat.

## Review / Deep Collection

Use for "写综述", "literature review", "state of the art", "evidence map".

Target 15-25 sources when time permits. Include:

- Inclusion/exclusion notes.
- L1-L4 layered list.
- Method or core argument summary.
- Source relationship map: who cites, updates, contradicts, or complements whom.
- Research gaps and search expansion terms.

## Materials

Use for "找素材", public writing, slides, article preparation, or narrative inputs.

Per source, extract:

- Strong data point or quotable claim, paraphrased unless a short direct quote is needed.
- Source identity and identifier.
- Why it matters for the narrative.
- Risk/caveat if the number is context-dependent.

Avoid backstage process language in public-facing Chinese drafts. Keep source provenance visible but readable.
